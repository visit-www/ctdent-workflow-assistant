🛡 How your patient data is protected

These are patient records, and you are the one answerable for them. So here is exactly what the app does with them, in plain English.

🚫☁️

Nothing is ever uploaded

No cloud. No account. No tracking. Your patient data never leaves your computer.

🔐

It all sits on your encrypted drive

Scans, case log, histories, everything the app keeps is in one folder, on the drive you chose.

📁

You choose that drive

The app has no default folder. Until you pick one, nothing is saved anywhere at all.

🚫☁️ Nothing is ever uploaded, there is nowhere to upload it to

The app has no server, no cloud storage, no account to sign up for, and no tracking of any kind. It does not phone home. It cannot see your cases, and neither can we.

All it does is drive your own browser to the CT Dent portal, the same portal you already use, and read the pages that come back. Your patient data goes from CT Dent to your computer, and stops there.

📁 You choose where everything is kept, and it must be an encrypted drive

The first time you run the app, it asks you to pick a folder. This is your Storage Target. It should be on a BitLocker-encrypted drive, internal or external, either is fine. "Encrypted" simply means that if someone steals the disk, its contents are unreadable without your login.

There is no default folder. The app will not download a single scan until you have told it where they should go.

🔐 Everything the app keeps lives in that one place

All of it goes on your encrypted drive, together:

  • The scans you download.
  • Your case log, the patient names and fees behind your month-end report.
  • The clinical histories the app fetches for the History drawer.
  • The browser profile. This one is easy to overlook: a browser quietly keeps copies of the pages it shows you, and those portal pages have patient names on them. So the app keeps its browser profile on your encrypted drive too.
  • Your month-end report (in ctdent-reports, with your own log and the redacted appraisal log kept apart).

An honest word about older versions. Version 2.1.3 and earlier got this wrong: some of the above, the case log, the histories, the downloaded zips and the browser profile, was left on your computer's C: drive, which may not be encrypted. Version 2.2.0 fixes it. When you first run it, it moves all of that onto your encrypted drive and deletes the old copies from C:. You do not need to do anything, but you deserved to be told.

✅ The app checks your drive for you

When you choose your folder, the app asks Windows whether that drive is actually encrypted with BitLocker. If it is not, it says so, loudly, before anything is saved there.

Sometimes Windows will not answer, that check normally needs administrator rights, and the app deliberately runs without them. When that happens the app tells you plainly that it could not confirm it, and asks you to check the drive yourself.

🔑 Your portal sign-in is protected

So that you are not asked for a one-time code every single time, the app saves your portal session, the "you are already signed in" pass the portal gives your browser.

It is scrambled using Windows' own built-in protection and locked to your Windows account. In practice: if that file is copied onto another computer, or opened by another person's Windows account, it is useless to them.

To be straight with you about the limit of that: it does not protect you against a virus or spyware that is already running on your own computer, signed in as you. Nothing stored on a computer can. Keep the machine locked and patched.

🙈 The app never asks for your CT Dent password

It does not ask for it, does not type it, and does not store it, anywhere. You sign in yourself, on the real portal page, in your own browser.

🗑 Uninstalling never quietly deletes patient records

When you remove the app, it asks you separately about your scans, your invoices and your exported reports. Every one of those questions is set to Keep by default, so a stray press of Enter can never destroy a patient record. Your files go only when you deliberately say so.

⚖️ What is still down to you

The app can only do its half. This half is yours:

  • Use an encrypted drive. BitLocker for an internal disk, BitLocker To Go for a USB or external one. This is required by your CT Dent agreement.
  • Lock your computer when you step away. Windows key + L.
  • Do not email scans around, and do not copy them onto unencrypted memory sticks.
  • Delete cases you no longer need. Keeping patient data longer than you need it is itself a breach.
  • Remember your position. As the reporting clinician you are the data controller under UK GDPR. This app is a tool you use; the responsibility for the data remains yours.

Scans you had already downloaded looked missing, fees you had already set went blank, and a patient could end up on your invoice twice. Cases are now matched on their Report Req ID, which does not change, and any duplicates already recorded are merged the first time you open this version. Check any invoice you issued recently.
  • Fixed in 2.3.6 — a case can never open another patient's scan. A rare consequence of the same problem: one patient's row could end up pointing at a scan belonging to someone else. The app now refuses that link and fetches the correct scan instead.

The app now writes them, encrypted with a passphrase you choose, to a folder you pick — OneDrive, a second drive, anywhere — every time it closes. If the drive is lost or the computer dies, Restore brings everything back. Only your passphrase opens it: keep it somewhere safe, because nobody can recover it for you.
  • Your case log survives a power cut. Saving the log used to empty the file before writing the new one, so losing power at the wrong moment could leave it blank. It is now written safely, keeps a spare copy, and will never overwrite a file it could not read.
  • Moving your storage folder no longer looks like losing everything. Choose a new folder and the app offers to bring your scans, case log and invoices across.
  • Invoices take other items. Add an expense or a credit — an encrypted drive you are recharging, say — as its own line. Months now read as July 2026 rather than 2026-07.
  • The appraisal log no longer names the referring clinician. Referral notes are signed, and those names were reaching a document meant to be shared. They are now removed along with the patient's.
  • Fixed in 2.3.3 — your worklist shows all of it. On a worklist long enough to run to a second page, the app could read just one page and report that as your whole list, and a case you could see on screen might refuse to open. It now checks the portal's own case count and tells you plainly if it ever comes up short.
  • New in 2.3.3 — reporting in one tab. Opening a case takes you straight to that patient's report page in the tab you are already in, instead of leaving a new tab behind every time. When you have finished, Done reporting brings you back to the worklist and refreshes just that patient, without re-reading the whole list.
  • Your scans are found again when the drive letter changes. An encrypted USB drive does not always come back as the same letter. The app now recognises your downloaded scans wherever the drive has landed, so it stops offering to fetch cases you already have.

What you need

The app brings no browser and no viewer of its own. It uses the ones you already have.

  • Windows 10 or 11 (64-bit)
  • Edge, Chrome or Brave
  • Your CT Dent portal login
  • A DICOM viewer, if you report in DICOM. Any one you like.
  • A secure folder for the scans. They are patient records.

Setting up

Once, and you are done.

  1. Run the installer. It installs for you alone, so Windows will not ask for an admin password.
  2. Choose where scans are saved. Pick a secure folder. Nothing is downloaded until you do.
  3. Pick your browser and your format. If you choose DICOM, the app asks which of your installed viewers should open the scans. Volume Viewer downloads bring their own.
  4. Click Start Session and sign in yourself, in the browser window that opens. You should only need to do this once: the app remembers the session, so next time you are straight in.

Questions people actually ask

The app shows one of these as a tip each day, under your worklist.

Can I keep the app on top of the browser?

Yes. Turn on "Pin on top" and the window stays above the browser and your viewer, so the worklist never disappears behind them.

The window is in my way. Can I see through it?

Yes. "Transparent" fades the window so you can read the scan or the portal straight through it, and "Side Strip" docks it as a slim bar down the edge of the screen.

Which browser does the app use?

Whichever you tell it to: Edge, Chrome or Brave. The app signs in to the portal using that browser, in a window of its own.

Which app opens my scans?

The one you pick. Click "Configure Viewers" to choose which viewer opens your DICOM scans, and change it whenever you like.

My browser or my viewer is not in the list.

The lists only show what is already installed on that computer. Install the browser or viewer you want first, then come back and change the setting. Nothing to reinstall.

Do I have to enter a one-time code every time?

No. Sign in on the CT Dent page once and the app remembers the session. After that, click "Start Session" and you are straight in.

Can I read the clinical history before opening a case?

Yes. Click "History" on the row. The app reads this from the portal in the background after each sync, one patient at a time, so give it a few minutes to arrive.

How do I know a patient has more than one study?

The background sync finds out for you. A patient with several studies gets one button per study on their row, and one £ button per study, so each is opened and priced separately.

Why did the £ button turn green?

Because a fee is recorded for that study. It stays editable: click the green button at any time to correct the amount.

What is in the month-end report?

Every study you opened, its fee, and its clinical summary, not just the amount. The spreadsheet still makes sense months later.

Can the app write my invoice?

Yes. One click builds the month's invoice as a PDF from the fees you recorded, and then offers to save, print or email it. You put your company details, bank details and invoice numbering into Advanced settings once, and the app does the rest. The invoice number advances by itself each time you save one.

I priced a case wrongly. Can I redo an invoice?

Yes. Correct the fee, then invoice that month again. The app replaces the same PDF, under the same number and the same file name. It will never create a second invoice for a month you may already have sent.

Does the invoice contain patient names?

No. It summarises the month per fee band, for example "12 x £65", with no names and no clinical details. It is a document that leaves your computer, so nothing identifiable goes on it.

Where do my scans and my password go?

The scans stay in the folder you chose, on your computer, and are never uploaded. The app never asks for your portal password, never types it and never stores it. You sign in yourself. How your data is protected →

Does my scan folder really have to be on an encrypted drive?

Yes. Your CT Dent agreement requires patient data to be held on an encrypted drive, and as the reporting clinician you are the data controller under UK GDPR. Use BitLocker on an internal disk, or BitLocker To Go on a USB or external one. When you choose your folder the app asks Windows whether that drive is encrypted, and warns you if it is not.

Does the app upload anything, anywhere?

No. There is no server, no cloud, no account and no tracking. The app only drives your own browser to the CT Dent portal you already use. Nothing about your patients is sent to us, because there is nowhere for it to be sent to.
